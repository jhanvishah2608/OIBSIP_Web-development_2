Dr. Avul Pakir Jainulabdeen Abdul Kalam (1931 – 2015)

About Him

Dr. APJ Abdul Kalam was one of India's greatest scientists and a beloved President. Born on October 15, 1931, in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu, he came from a humble background but rose to become the most respected figure in Indian science and politics. He inspired millions of young people to dream big and work hard.

Life Timeline

  • 1931 Born in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu
  • 1954 Graduated in Physics from Saint Joseph's College
  • 1960 Joined DRDO as a scientist
  • 1969 Moved to ISRO, worked on satellite launch vehicles
  • 1983 Led India's Integrated Guided Missile Development Programme
  • 1998 Key role in Pokhran-II nuclear tests
  • 2002 Became the 11th President of India
  • 2007 Retired from Presidency
  • 2015 Passed away on July 27 while delivering a lecture in Shillong

Awards & Honours

Bharat Ratna (1997) Padma Vibhushan (1990) Padma Bhushan (1981) Indira Gandhi Award Veer Savarkar Award Ramanujan Award
